    <h2 class="ArtP-subheadline">This year’s pace of deaths, however, is lower than during the past four years.
 </h2>


                            

                            

                            <div class="ArtP-articleContainer">
                                
                                    <div class="ArtP-articleBody">
                                        
<p>The number of Florida resident deaths this year linked to COVID-19 is nearing 3,000, according to data posted on the state Department of Health website.
</p>
<p>The data Monday showed 2,972 reported deaths related to COVID-19, up from 2,740 in early June.
</p>
<p>This year’s pace of deaths, however, is lower than during the past four years.
</p>
<p>The pandemic hit the state in 2020. That year, Florida had a reported 23,345 deaths, according to state data.
</p>
<p>The number jumped to 39,867 in 2021, before declining to 21,292 in 2022 and 8,439 in 2023.
</p><p>This year, 262 of the reported deaths have been in Miami-Dade County, while 231 have been in Palm Beach County. </p><p>
