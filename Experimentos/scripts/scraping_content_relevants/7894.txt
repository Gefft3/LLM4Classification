causes vomiting and diarrhea. It can often be confused with the “stomach flu” or “stomach bug”. However, Norovirus is not related to the flu, which is caused by the influenza virus.</p><p>The&nbsp;<a href="https://www.cdc.gov/norovirus/outbreaks/index.html" target="_blank">Center for Disease Control</a>&nbsp;(CDC) recently warned about norovirus outbreaks nationwide.</p><div class="in-article-oop-container"><div class="tsm-ad tsm-ad-oop" aria-label="advertisement"><div id="tsm-ad-in-article-oop-1"><!-- dynamic ad --></div></div><div class="tsm-ad tsm-ad-1x1 tsm-ad-oop" aria-label="advertisement"><div id="tsm-ad-in-article-pixel-1"><!-- dynamic ad --></div></div></div><h3><a href="https://bigfrog104.com/polly-debunking-flu-shot-myths/" target="_blank"><span style="color: #000000;">READ MORE:</span><span style="color: #ff0000;">&nbsp;Debunking Flu Myths</span></a></h3><div class="single-post-image "><figure class="img-tag has-sizer aligncenter" style="max-width:1423px"><div class="frame-sizer" style="padding-top:74.98243148278286%"></div><div class="theframe" data-image="https://townsquare.media/site/39/files/2024/02/attachment-RS51194_GettyImages-158804734-scr.jpg" rel=""><span class="visually-hidden">selvanegra/Think Stock</span></div><figcaption>selvanegra/Think Stock</figcaption><div class="loadinganim showbox" id="0"><div class="loader" style="width:30px;height:30px"><svg class="circular" viewBox="25 25 50 50" style="width:30px;height:30px" focusable="false" role="img" aria-labelledby="loading-anim-title"><title id="loading-anim-title">loading...</title><circle class="path" cx="50" cy="50" r="20" fill="none" stroke-width="4" stroke-miterlimit="10"></circle></svg></div></div></figure><div></div></div><h2>Outbreak at Colgate University</h2><p>The <a href="https://www.madisoncounty.ny.gov/CivicAlerts.aspx?AID=464" target="_blank">Madison County Public Health Department</a> is investigating after 15 Colgate University students started having symptoms. Two tested positive for the highly contagious virus. Another 54 people in Hamilton County may have it too.</p><blockquote><p>The majority of suspect cases are associated with attendance at different social events held on and off campus. The first confirmed case attended a fundraising event at the Colgate Inn on February 22, 2024 and began symptoms after.</p></blockquote><p>More people are expected to test positive as the investigation continues over the next few days.</p><h3><a href="https://bigfrog104.com/top-ten-colleges-in-new-york/" target="_blank"><span style="color: #000000;">READ MORE:</span> <span style="color: #ff0000;">Colgate University Among Top 10 in Country</span></a></h3><p>The University is working with Madison County Public Health to identify more people who may be sick and enhance cleaning to slow the spread on campus.</p><div class="single-post-image "><figure class="img-tag has-sizer aligncenter" style="max-width:2048px"><div class="frame-sizer" style="padding-top:66.50390625%"></div><div class="theframe" data-image="https://townsquare.media/site/39/files/2014/08/10514241_10154338142720181_1475481192760737400_o.jpg" rel=""><span class="visually-hidden">Colgate</span></div><figcaption>Colgate</figcaption><div class="loadinganim showbox" id="0"><div class="loader" style="width:30px;height:30px"><svg class="circular" viewBox="25 25 50 50" style="width:30px;height:30px" focusable="false" role="img" aria-labelledby="loading-anim-title"><title id="loading-anim-title">loading...</title><circle class="path" cx="50" cy="50" r="20" fill="none" stroke-width="4" stroke-miterlimit="10"></circle></svg></div></div></figure><div></div></div><h2>Avoid Getting or Spreading Norovirus</h2><ul><li>STAY HOME if you are sick. To keep norovirus to yourself, do not attend school, work, social functions, or activities until 48 hours after your symptoms have ended.</li><li>WASH YOUR HANDS. Thoroughly rub hands with soap and water for 20 seconds after using the bathroom, sneezing or coughing, or having contact with soiled surfaces or items. Hand sanitizers<strong>&nbsp;are not usually effective against norovirus</strong>.</li><li>Wash fruits and vegetables and cook shellfish thoroughly before eating.</li><li>Do NOT prepare or serve food or care for others when sick with norovirus for at least 48 hours after symptoms have ended.</li><li>Do NOT share dining items, such as utensils and cups.</li><li>Clean and disinfect contaminated surfaces or objects with a bleach solution several times daily. To make a bleach solution daily, use 3/4 cup concentrated bleach (or 1 cup of regular strength bleach) to one gallon of water. To disinfect, leave the surface wet with the bleach solution for at least 5 minutes and then rinse thoroughly with clean water.</li></ul><p>To learn more about norovirus, visit the <a href="https://www.cdc.gov/norovirus/index.html" target="_blank">CDC</a>.</p><p>Think you have/had norovirus?</p><p>Contact the Madison County Health Department if you experienced vomiting, stomach pain, and diarrhea within 12-48 hours AND attended an event in the Town of Hamilton or had close contact with someone with the same symptoms in the Town of Hamilton.</p><div class="photogallery-wrapper blog-photogallery "><h2 class="photogallery-title">Norovirus Symptoms New Yorkers Need To Beware Of</h2><div class="photogallery-description"><div>The CDC is warning New Yorkers about a possible Norovirus outbreak.
