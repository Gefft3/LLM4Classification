</div></div></div><div class="basic-page-description"><div class="basic-page-description-metabox-title">Content Overview</div><div class="basic-page-description-text">Yellow fever, Zika, polio, malaria, measles, Lyme, cholera, chikungunya, and dengue disease vaccinations in 2024.</div></div><div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><h2>Vax-Before-Travel&nbsp;Vaccines 2025</h2>
<p>In the past few decades, <a href="https://journals.asm.org/doi/10.1128/jcm.00910-24?utm_source=literatum&amp;utm_medium=email&amp;utm_campaign=alert-emails">viruses</a> transmitted by mosquitoes have increasingly spread across the world, causing significant disease <a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/travel-diseases">outbreaks</a> in naÃ¯ve populations. Recent&nbsp;<a href="https://academic.oup.com/ofid/advance-article/doi/10.1093/ofid/ofae761/7945119">research</a>&nbsp;says tens of millions of people are not appropriately immunized against diseases&nbsp;before&nbsp;visiting endemic countries. Getting&nbsp;<a href="https://www.who.int/travel-advice/vaccines" target="_blank">vaccinated</a>&nbsp;against&nbsp;<a href="https://www.nfid.org/infectious-diseases/" target="_blank">infectious diseases</a>&nbsp;is one of the most effective ways to protect your health while traveling abroad, says the World Health Organization (<a href="https://www.who.int/teams/immunization-vaccines-and-biologicals/diseases" target="_blank">WHO</a>). Since 1999, the WHO's Strategic Advisory Group of Experts on Immunization has provided high-level&nbsp;<a href="https://www.mdpi.com/2076-393X/12/12/1402?utm_campaign=releaseissue_vaccinesutm_medium=emailutm_source=releaseissueutm_term=titlelink46" target="_blank">recommendations</a>&nbsp;on vaccines and immunization and&nbsp;updated its&nbsp;<a href="https://extranet.who.int/pqweb/vaccines" target="_blank">list</a>&nbsp;of vaccines in 2025.</p>
<p>According to the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (<a href="https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/traveler-information-center">CDC</a>), most&nbsp;<a href="https://www.hhs.gov/immunization/who-and-when/travel/index.html">travel vaccines</a>&nbsp;should&nbsp;be administered at least one month&nbsp;before departure&nbsp;to ensure maximum&nbsp;protection. The CDC lists the <a href="https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/yellowbook/2024/preparing/vaccination-and-immunoprophylaxis-general-principles">minimum ages and&nbsp;intervals</a> between doses for&nbsp;travel vaccines recommended for&nbsp;U.S. residents. Various U.S. Food and Drug (<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/covid-19-vaccines-usa">FDA</a>), <a href="https://www.gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice" target="_blank">U.K. Foreign Travel Advice</a>, and European Medicines Agency (<a href="https://twitter.com/ema_news">EMA</a>) vaccines are approved for international travelers.</p>
<p>To better inform travelers, the Vax-Before-Travel&nbsp;<a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/vaccines" target="_blank">vaccine library</a>&nbsp;was updated in January&nbsp;2025, on-demand lab testing services are available at <a href="https://www.ultalabtests.com/partners/precisionvaccinations">Ulta Lab Testing</a>, and vaccine appointment requests can be submitted at this Vax-Before-Travel <a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/vax-before-travel/appointment">link</a>. Vaccine certificates, digital passports, and enhanced visa information for 2025 are&nbsp;posted at this VBT <a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/travel-vaccine-certificates-passports-visa">link</a>.</p>
<h2>Travel Vaccine Advisories</h2>
<p>The U.S. CDC and the <a href="https://travelhealthpro.org.uk/factsheet/67/vaccines-and-medicines-availability-supply-shortages-and-use-of-unlicensed-medicines">U.K. Travel Health Pro</a>, the Pan American Health Organization (<a href="https://opendata.paho.org/en" target="_blank">PAHO</a>), and the European Centers for Disease Control (<a href="https://www.ecdc.europa.eu/en/travellers-health" target="_blank">ECDC</a>) publish&nbsp;<a href="https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/notices">Travel Health Advisories</a>&nbsp;<a href="https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/travelers/international-travel/index.html" target="_blank">Assessments</a>, including&nbsp;guidance for&nbsp;<a href="https://www.cdc.gov/quarantine/maritime/guidance-ARI.html" target="_blank">cruise ships</a>, enabling international travelers to confirm vaccine <a href="https://cdn.who.int/media/docs/default-source/travel-and-health/vaccination-requirements-and-who-recommendations-ith-2022-country-list.pdf?sfvrsn=be429f2_1&amp;download=true" target="_blank">recommendations</a>&nbsp;by country. The <a href="https://travel.state.gov/content/travel.html">U.S. Department of State</a> publishes Travel Advisories, and U.S. embassies issue <a href="https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/traveladvisories/traveladvisories.html/">travel health security notices</a>. Seperately,&nbsp; &nbsp;<a href="https://healthmap.org/en/" target="_blank">Healthmap.org</a>&nbsp; publishes disease outbreaks that are segmented by country.&nbsp;</p>
<p>As of January 2025,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/travel-vaccine-certificates-and-passports">Travel Vaccine Certificates and&nbsp;Passport</a> information is posted at this link.</p>
<h2>Anthrax Vaccines</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/cyfendus-av7909-anthrax-vaccine">CYFENDUS â„¢</a> (AV7909, BioThraxÂ®), a two-dose anthrax vaccine for Post-Exposure Prophylaxis, was approved on July 20, 2023.</p>
<h2>Avian Influenza Vaccines</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/audenz-avian-influenza-vaccine">Audenzâ„¢</a> is a monovalent, adjuvanted,&nbsp;cell-based, inactivated&nbsp;subunit vaccine&nbsp;<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/avian-influenza-vaccines" target="_blank">approved</a>&nbsp;by the U.S. FDA. Various&nbsp;<a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/avian-influenza-vaccines">pandemic influenza vaccines</a> have also been approved in Europe and the U.K.</p>
<h2>Chikungunya&nbsp;Vaccines</h2>
<p>As of 2025,&nbsp;one U.S. FDA-approved&nbsp;<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/chikungunya-vaccines-2023" target="_blank">chikungunya vaccine</a>, IXCHIQÂ® (<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/vla1553-chikungunya-vaccine" target="_blank">VLA1553</a>), is&nbsp;available in the U.S. Chikungunya&nbsp;<a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/chikungunya-outbreaks" target="_blank">outbreaks</a>&nbsp;are impacting about 100 countries.</p>
<h2>Cholera Vaccine</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/cholera-vaccines-2023">Cholera</a> vaccine&nbsp;<a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/cholera-vaccine-supplies-increase-so-do-fatalities-2024-12-18">availability</a> improved in late 2024. WHO-prequalified oral cholera vaccines, including DukoralÂ®, Shancholâ„¢, and EuvicholÂ®, are available for international travelers.&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/vaxchora-cholera-vaccine">Vaxchora</a>&nbsp;is&nbsp;an oral cholera vaccine for active immunization against disease caused by&nbsp;Vibrio cholerae&nbsp;serogroup O1.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/dukoral-cholera-vaccine">DUKORAL</a>Â® is available in Europe, the U.K., and various countries.&nbsp;</p>
<h2>Dengue&nbsp;Vaccines</h2>
<p>As of 2025, various countries have approved&nbsp;<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/qdenga-tak-003-dengue-vaccine">QDENGAÂ®</a>,&nbsp;a&nbsp;tetravalent dengue vaccine. Dengue <a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/dengue-vaccines">vaccine candidates</a> are conducting late-stage studies in 2025.</p>
<h2>Diphtheria Vaccines</h2>
<p>The U.S. CDC says&nbsp;travelers two months and older who are traveling to outbreak areas should receive an age-appropriate dose of the diphtheria toxoid-containing vaccine&nbsp;if they are not fully vaccinated&nbsp;or have not received a booster dose within five years before departure. In 2024,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.cdc.gov/vaccines/vpd/dtap-tdap-td/public/index.html#should-get-vaccines" target="_blank">11 vaccines will be</a>&nbsp;available to help protect against diphtheria.</p>
<h2>Ebola&nbsp;Vaccines</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/ebola-outbreaks">Ebola</a> outbreaks in Africa began in 1976 and continued in 2024. Zaire Ebolavirus vaccines have&nbsp;limitedly available outside of Africa.&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/vaccines/ervebo-ebola-vaccine">Ervebo</a>,&nbsp;Ebola Zaire Vaccine, Live, is a&nbsp;recombinant, replication-competent Ebola vaccine.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/zabdeno-ad26zebov-and-mvabea-mva-bn-filo-ebola-vaccine">Zabdeno</a> (Ad26.ZEBOV) and Mvabea (MVA-BN-Filo) are an&nbsp;<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/zabdeno-mvabea-ebola-vaccine">Ebola</a>&nbsp;vaccine therapy.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/ebanga-ebola-antibody">Ebangaâ„¢</a> (mAb114,&nbsp;Ansuvimab-zykl)&nbsp;is&nbsp;a human monoclonal antibody approved for treating&nbsp;Zaire ebolavirus&nbsp;infections.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/ebola-vaccines-2023">Sudan Ebolavirus</a> vaccines are being developed in clinical trials.</p>
<h2>Influenza Vaccines</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/vaccines/influenza-vaccines">Flu shots</a> are recommended for international travelers wherever&nbsp;influenza viruses spread.</p>
<h2>Japanese Encephalitis&nbsp;Vaccines</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/jenvac-japanese-encephalitis-vaccine">JENVAC</a>&nbsp;is a single-dose inactivated Japanese Encephalitis&nbsp;Vaccine. This Vero cell-derived vaccine is prepared from the virus's&nbsp;Indian strain (Kolar- 821564XYs).</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/ixiaro-japanese-encephalitis-vaccine">Ixiaro</a> is an inactivated, adsorbed&nbsp;Vero cell culture-derived vaccine targeted against the Japanese encephalitis virus. It is prepared by propagating JEV strain SA14-14-2 in Vero cells.</p>
<h2>Lassa Fever Vaccine</h2>
<p>Lassa fever&nbsp;is an acute viral hemorrhagic fever without an approved <a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/lassa-fever-vaccine">vaccine</a> in 2024.</p>
<h2>Lyme Disease Vaccines</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/lyme-disease-vaccines">Lyme disease</a> vaccine candidates are conducting&nbsp;late-stage clinical studies. Valneva's&nbsp;<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/lyme-disease-vaccine-vla15">VLA15</a> is a&nbsp;multivalent recombinant protein&nbsp;vaccine candidate.</p>
<h2>Malaria Vaccines</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/malaria-outbreaks">Malaria</a> outbreaks continue in 2025, and <a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/malaria-vaccines">vaccines</a> are available in Africa but not in the U.S.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/malaria-mosquirix-rtssas01e-vaccine">Mosquirix</a> (RTS,S/AS01e) is a recombinant vaccine that triggers the immune system to defend against the first stages of infections when the Plasmodium falciparum malaria parasite enters the human host's bloodstream through a mosquito bite.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/r21-matrix-m-malaria-vaccine">R21/Matrix-Mâ„¢</a>&nbsp;Malaria vaccine is produced by the&nbsp;Serum Institute of India and developed by scientists at the University of Oxford in England.</p>
<h2>Marburg Disease Vaccines</h2>
<p>Marburg vaccine <a href="https://www.vaxbeforetravel.com/marburg-vaccine-2023">candidates</a> are conducting clinical trials, and various Marburg disease <a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/marburg-disease-outbreaks">outbreaks</a> have been reported in 2024.</p>
<h2>Measles&nbsp;Vaccines</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/measles-outbreaks-2023">Measles outbreaks</a> continue in 2024, including in U.S. cities in Chicago and Minneapolis. Various measles <a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/measles-vaccines">vaccines</a> are available at pharmacies.</p>
<h2>MERS Vaccine</h2>
<p>As of 2025, no approved <a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/mers-vaccines-2023">MERS-CoV vaccine</a>&nbsp;exists, but cases continue to be reported in the <a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/camels-shed-more-mers-2023-10-13">Middle East</a>. The&nbsp;<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/vtp-500-mers-vaccine">VTP-500</a> vaccine candidate completed Phase I clinical trials in Britain and Saudi Arabia, and the University of Oxford a&nbsp;Phase Ib trial&nbsp;in the U.K. to assess the vaccination of older adults.</p>
<h2>Norovirus Vaccine</h2>
<p>As of 2025, the U.S. FDA has not approved a <a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/norovirus-vaccines">norovirus vaccine</a> candidate, but Moderna's vaccine is conducting a phase 3 clinical trial. The Nova 301 Phase 3 study evaluating the efficacy, safety, and immunogenicity of <a href="https://trials.modernatx.com/study/?id=mRNA-1403-P301">mRNA-1403</a> for adults is expected to be completed in 2027.</p>
<h2>Mpox Vaccines</h2>
<p>The&nbsp;<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/jynneos-smallpox-vaccine">JYNNEOS</a> smallpox-mpox&nbsp;vaccine is commercially available in the U.S. Other mpox vaccines are available in Africa.</p>
<h2>Nipah Virus Vaccines</h2>
<p>Nipah virus <a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/nipah-virus-vaccine">vaccine candidates</a> continue in phase 1 clinical trials in 2023. Since 1999, Nipah&nbsp;outbreaks&nbsp;have occurred&nbsp;in Asia, including&nbsp;Bangladesh&nbsp;and&nbsp;India.</p>
<h2>Oropouche Virus Vaccine</h2>
<p>As of 2025, there are <a href="https://www.cdc.gov/oropouche/hcp/clinical-overview/index.html">no</a>&nbsp;Oropouche virus vaccines available.</p>
<h2>Plague Vaccine</h2>
<p>The <a href="https://cdn.who.int/media/docs/default-source/documents/r-d-blueprint-meetings/global-consultation-on-plague-vaccines/2_ashok-chopra_who-plague-vaccines-2023f-11.pdf?sfvrsn=9b9e5f23_4">WHO-Plague Vaccines</a> in Preclinical Development and Clinical Trials was published in 2023. The primary outcomes were efficacy, safety, and immunogenicity, assessed using the Cochrane Collaborations tool. The <a href="https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.adg1036">study </a>concluded that a&nbsp;single-dose F1-based mRNA-LNP vaccine protects the lethal plague bacterium.</p>
<h2>Polio&nbsp;Vaccines</h2>
<p>Polio vaccination, including booster shots, when visiting <a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/polio-outbreaks">polio-endemic countries</a>.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/ipol-poliovirus-vaccine" target="_blank">IPOL</a>&nbsp;is a sterile suspension of three types of poliovirus: Type 1 (Mahoney), Type 2 (MEF-1), and Type 3 (Saukett). Sanofi Pasteur's single-antigen IPOL vaccine is a highly purified, inactivated poliovirus vaccine with enhanced potency.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/sabin-ipv-polio-vaccine" target="_blank">Sabin Inactivated Poliovirus Vaccine</a> is a liquid trivalent vaccine produced&nbsp;from Sabin poliovirus type 1, 2, and 3 strains grown on Vero cells.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/nopv2-polio-vaccine">nOPV2</a>&nbsp;polio vaccine is derived from the live, infectious virus, but it has been 'triple-locked using genetic engineering to prevent it from becoming harmful. nOPV2&nbsp;is genetically more stable than existing OPVs.</p>
<h2>Rabies&nbsp;Vaccines</h2>
<p>Various <a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/rabies-vaccines">rabies vaccines</a> and candidates seek to reduce rabies mortality in 2025.</p>

<p>RMSF is <a href="https://emergency.cdc.gov/han/2023/han00502.asp">endemic</a> in multiple border states in northern Mexico, including but not exclusive, to Baja California, Sonora, Chihuahua, Coahuila, and Nuevo LeÃ³n. <h2>Rotavirus Vaccines</h2>
<p>Since 2019,&nbsp;the WHO has prequalified four&nbsp;<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/rotavirus-vaccines" target="_blank">rotavirus vaccines</a>. GSK's&nbsp;<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/rotarix-rotavirus-vaccine">Rotarix</a>&nbsp;is a live, attenuated rotavirus vaccine that exposes your child to a small dose of the virus, which causes the body to develop immunity to the disease.</p>
<h2>Tick-Borne Encephalitis Vaccine</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/ticovac-vaccine">TicoVac</a>&nbsp;vaccine&nbsp;is marketed by Pfizer Inc. under the brand names FSME-ImmunÂ®&nbsp;in Europe and TICOVACâ„¢ in the U.S. It was&nbsp;developed using a master 'seed' virus similar to the tick-borne encephalitis virus found in nature.</p>
<h2>Tuberculosis Vaccine</h2>
<p>The&nbsp;<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/bacille-calmette-guerin-bcg-vaccine">BCG&nbsp;vaccine</a>&nbsp;helps&nbsp;prevent tuberculosis&nbsp;and is used for nonspecific protective effects. Various versions of the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/tuberculosis-vaccines-2023">BCG vaccine</a>&nbsp;are available globally.&nbsp;</p>
<h2>Typhoid Vaccine</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/typhoid-vaccines">Typhoid vaccines</a> are available in 2023 and are recommended for people traveling to places where typhoid fever is common, such as South Asia&nbsp;(India).</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/vivotif-typhoid-vaccine">Vivotif oral vaccine</a>&nbsp;(capsules) is indicated for the immunization of adults and children over six years of age against disease caused by&nbsp;Salmonella&nbsp;Typhi. It contains live bacteria called&nbsp;Salmonella typhi&nbsp;strain Ty21a, which does not cause typhoid fever. Bavarian Nordic A/S owns Vivotif Oral&nbsp;and is available in the U.S.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/typbar-tcv-typhoid-vaccine">Typbar TCV</a>&nbsp;is a vaccine containing polysaccharide of&nbsp;Salmonella typhi Ty2&nbsp;conjugated to Tetanus Toxoid.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/typhim-vi-typhoid-vaccine">Typhim VI</a>&nbsp;is a sterile solution prepared from the purified polysaccharide capsule of Salmonella typhi (Ty 2 strain).&nbsp;</p>
<h2>Urinary Track Infection Vaccine and Treatments</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/uromune-mv140-urinary-tract-infection-vaccine">Uromuneâ„¢</a> Urinary Tract Infection (UTI) inactivated, oral spray vaccine is approved in various countries.&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/pivya-pivmecillinam-tablets-urinary-tract-infections-uti">Pivyaâ„¢</a>&nbsp;antibacterial tablet is approved for female adults with uncomplicated UTIs&nbsp;in Europe.</p>
<h2>Yellow Fever&nbsp;Vaccines</h2>
<p>The WHO publishes&nbsp;<a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/yellow-fever-vaccines">yellow fever</a> vaccine&nbsp;<a href="https://www.who.int/publications/m/item/vaccination-requirements-and-recommendations-for-international-travellers-and-malaria-situation-per-country-2022-edition">requirements</a>&nbsp;for entering certain countries.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/yf-vax-yellow-fever-vaccine">YF-VAX</a>Â®&nbsp;vaccine is&nbsp;licensed in the U.S. and requires about ten days to produce maximum immunity.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/vaccines/stamaril-yellow-fever-vaccine">Stamaril</a>Â®&nbsp;is&nbsp;distributed in over 70 countries in 2024,&nbsp;but not in the U.S.&nbsp;</p>

<p>While Zika virus <a href="https://www.vax-before-travel.com/zika-outbreaks">outbreaks</a> continue primarily in India and the Region of the Americas in 2024, no approved <a href="https://www.precisionvaccinations.com/zika-vaccines#:~:text=Zika%20Vaccine%20Candidates%202023,the%20Japanese%20Encephalitis%20vaccine%20IXIARO.">Zika vaccine</a>&nbsp;is&nbsp;available.</p>
