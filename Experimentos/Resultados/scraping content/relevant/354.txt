            <div class="rich-text"><div class="rich-text"><p>A mosquito sample collected in Stanislaus County has tested positive for St. Louis Encephalitis virus, according to the area’s two mosquito abatement districts.<br></p><p>Like West Nile Virus, most people who become infected with SLEV will never feel sick. Most people who do feel sick will have mild flu-like symptoms; a small number of people will have severe disease with headache, confusion, disorientation and dizziness. Seizures, paralysis, coma and sometimes death may occur. Severe disease is more likely in people who are older and those with weakened immune systems.</p><p>SLEV is related to the West Nile Virus and is transmitted via the bite of Culex mosquitoes, the same mosquitoes that transmit WNV.</p><div class="in-text-ad">
<div data-collapse-exclude="">
<div data-component="DynamicAdSlot" data-prop-ad-unit="anvil-rectangle" data-prop-dfp-network-id="30576467" data-prop-size-mapping="[{&quot;viewport&quot;: [0, 0], &quot;sizes&quot;: [[300, 250], [250, 250]]}]" data-prop-sizes="[[300, 250], [250, 250]]" data-prop-start-collapsed="False" data-prop-targeting-arguments="{&quot;article_body&quot;: true, &quot;page_type&quot;: &quot;article page&quot;, &quot;page_id&quot;: 255518, &quot;section&quot;: &quot;Local&quot;, &quot;rate_card&quot;: &quot;Default Rate Card&quot;, &quot;site&quot;: &quot;www.cerescourier.com&quot;, &quot;uri&quot;: &quot;/news/local/mosquito-borne-encephalitis-virus-found-stanislaus-county/&quot;}"></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>“The discovery of positive mosquito pools is a reminder that we need to continuously prevent mosquito breeding in our community. There is no specific treatment for SLEV or WNV, so it is very important that people protect themselves and their families from mosquito bites,” advises Dr. Julie Vaishampayan, Stanislaus County Public Health Officer. “We ask everyone to use mosquito repellents to protect themselves and their loved ones from mosquito-borne viruses, especially when they are outdoors.”</p><p>Statewide, there have been 26 mosquito samples that have tested positive for SLEV this year. They are from six counties including Fresno, Imperial, Madera, Riverside, Stanislaus and Tulare. There have been no reports of SLEV in people this year. During 2020, there were four reports of people with SLEV in California; one of these was from Stanislaus County.</p><p>WNV detection in mosquitoes is increasing; a total of 133 mosquito samples have tested positive this year in Stanislaus County. WNV has also been confirmed in two dead birds.</p><p>The districts anticipate more SLEV and WNV detections in the coming months and would like to remind residents they can help by taking the following precautions:</p><p>• Dump or drain standing water. These are places mosquitoes like to lay their eggs.</p><div class="in-text-ad">
<div data-collapse-exclude="">
<div data-component="DynamicAdSlot" data-prop-ad-unit="anvil-rectangle" data-prop-dfp-network-id="30576467" data-prop-size-mapping="[{&quot;viewport&quot;: [0, 0], &quot;sizes&quot;: [[300, 250], [250, 250]]}]" data-prop-sizes="[[300, 250], [250, 250]]" data-prop-start-collapsed="False" data-prop-targeting-arguments="{&quot;article_body&quot;: true, &quot;page_type&quot;: &quot;article page&quot;, &quot;page_id&quot;: 255518, &quot;section&quot;: &quot;Local&quot;, &quot;rate_card&quot;: &quot;Default Rate Card&quot;, &quot;site&quot;: &quot;www.cerescourier.com&quot;, &quot;uri&quot;: &quot;/news/local/mosquito-borne-encephalitis-virus-found-stanislaus-county/&quot;}"></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>• Defend yourself against mosquitoes by using repellants containing DEET, Picaridin, or Oil of</p><p>Lemon Eucalyptus.</p><p>• Avoid being outdoors at dusk and dawn. These are the times when WNV carrying mosquitoes</p><p>are generally most active.</p><p>• Report Neglected Swimming pools to your local mosquito abatement district.</p><p>• Use tight-fitting door and window screens to keep mosquitoes from entering your home.</p><p>For additional information or to request service, residents should contact their local District: Stanislaus County residents:</p><p>— North of the Tuolumne River contact: Eastside Mosquito Abatement District at (209) 522-4098 (www.eastsidemosquito.com);</p><p>— South of the Tuolumne River contact: Turlock Mosquito Abatement District at (209) 634-1234 (www.turlockmosquito.org).</p></div></div>
