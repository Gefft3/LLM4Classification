<span class="posted-on"><a href="https://blueprint.ng/lassa-fever-outbreak-hits-gombe-governor-declares-preventive-measures/" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2024-01-18T06:35:42+01:00">January 18, 2024</time><time class="updated" datetime="2024-01-18T06:36:25+01:00">January 18, 2024</time></a></span><span class="meta-sep"> - </span><span class="byline"> by <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="">Auwal Ahmad</a></span></span></div></header>
<a class="image-link" href="https://blueprint.ng/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/gombe-state-Inuwa-1.jpg"><img width="273" height="184" src="https://blueprint.ng/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/gombe-state-Inuwa-1.jpg" class="attachment-hitmag-featured size-hitmag-featured wp-post-image" alt="" decoding="async"></a><div class="entry-content"><p>&nbsp;</p><p>Gombe state Commissioner of Health, Dr. Habu Dahiru, has announced the unfortunate occurrence of the first outbreak of Lassa fever in the state, urging widespread information sharing to curtail the situation promptly.</p><p>Dr. Dahiru said cases in the state over the past five years have created concerns. He said in 2018, three cases were reported while in 2019 and 2020, the number rose to six cases each whereas there were no reported cases in 2021. However, in 2023, the state faced a surge with 24 suspected cases.</p><p>The commissioner disclosed that the first confirmed case in 2024 was a 25-year-old driver who had recently travelled to Adamawa and spent two weeks there. Upon his return, he exhibited symptoms such as fever, headache, and abdominal pain. Initially admitted to a local hospital, the deteriorating condition prompted a referral to the Teaching Hospital. A Lassa test was conducted, and the patient was confirmed positive.</p><p>According to him, a systematic approach involving case identification, isolation, and treatment has been activated. The health system has implemented a laboratory pillar for the collection of samples from suspected cases for further investigation.</p><p>The commissioner added that a risk communication pillar has been established to educate the public on the transmission of Lassa fever, emphasising that the virus is primarily transmitted through rats, which harbour and spread the infection. He cautioned against improper food handling practices, such as allowing rats to contaminate stored food products or leaving utensils exposed overnight.</p><p>The commissioner stressed that collective efforts were essential to contain the outbreak and protect the health of the community.</p><div class="simplesocialbuttons simplesocial-simple-round simplesocialbuttons_inline simplesocialbuttons-align-left post-582440 post  simplesocialbuttons-inline-no-animation">
