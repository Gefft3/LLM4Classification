<h1 class="nam_title wp-block-post-title has-x-large-font-size">Namibia 83% ready for polio outbreak</h1>

<div class="entry-content post_content wp-block-post-content is-layout-flow wp-block-post-content-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong><em>Executive Director</em> of health and social services Ben Nangombe says an evaluation of the country’s preparedness for a polio outbreak has revealed the country is 83% ready.</strong></p>



<p>He says this has doubled since the last evaluation conducted in 2018.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Nangombe announced this at the closing of a three-day polio outbreak response simulation exercise in Windhoek.</p><p class="ps-flex ps-justify-center">
							<!-- In Article 2 -->
							<ins class="adsbygoogle" style="display:inline-block;width:728px;height:90px;" data-ad-client="ca-pub-2889986781052758" data-ad-slot="4472778619"></ins>
							<script>(adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});</script></p>



<p>He applauded the team that participated in the simulation.&nbsp;</p>



<p>“This is more than the global average, which is 80%,” Nangombe said.&nbsp;</p>



<p>He emphasised the importance of being able to timely detect and promptly respond to any polio outbreak in Namibia.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Nangombe said preparedness is tested through an actual outbreak, but in the absence of this, simulation exercises are used.</p>



<p>“This is exactly what we we’ve been doing here these last three days – conducting a polio outbreak simulation exercise to test our system . . . if we have what it takes to respond and contain a polio outbreak,” he said.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Poliomyelitis causes permanent disability and death, which is why eradicating it is a priority, Nangombe said.&nbsp;</p>



<p>He said the oral vaccine may cause polio and that there have been cases of vaccine-derived polio viruses recorded by environmental surveillance in Botswana.</p>
